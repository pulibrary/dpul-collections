## Audience

In the development of Digital Collections, our goals are for the site to be:
- Useful for the researchers with whom our subject librarians and collection assistants liaise
- Accessible to first year undergraduate students
- Enjoyable to individuals who have an interest in that material
- A product that our developers and stakeholders feel proud of

The following audience summaries and personas are one step in an iterative process of defining our primary audiences and their core tasks and motivations.


### Researchers

Researchers are frequently retrieving known items or collections, or searching within resources for a known topic.

Example "known search" researcher use cases:
- How can I find all mentions of the word "sugar" in this collection?
- I want to go to my known collection link to see what new things have been added
- Please send me all the Chilean posters from the 1980s and 90s

Researchers may encounter other topics or collections in the site that they find interesting, but are less central to their focus area of expertise. This may lead to exploratory use of the site.

Researchers may be motivated by:
- Pursuit of long-term/ongoing research goals
- Distributing materials to students and teaching needs
- Analysis of a full body of work using DH techniques


### Content partners

PUL works with a variety of academic and cultural experts to collect, preserve, and provide access to digitized materials. Often these partners collaborate with us to provide digitization and/or metadata for these materials.

Example use cases:
- I need to send researchers I'm working with to a jumping-off point to working with these materials


### First-year undergraduate student

A first-year undergraduate student is unfamiliar with the specific areas in which PUL collects, and the reasons we may digitize certain materials. 

Example exploratory search use cases:
- Does PUL collect and digitize materials relevant to my interests? To my cultural or ethnic identity?
- What materials are here that might be interesting to me as a research topic?
